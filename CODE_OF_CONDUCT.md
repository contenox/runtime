# Code of Conduct

Contenox is a place to build practical, auditable AI workflow tooling. The community works best when people can bring bugs, rough ideas, questions, and careful criticism without needing to perform expertise first.

## How We Work Together

Be direct, but keep it useful. Explain what you saw, what you expected, and what tradeoff you think matters.

Assume imperfect information. A bug report may be incomplete, a feature idea may be rough, and a maintainer answer may need follow-up.

Respect time and context. Search when it is practical, link what you know, and keep threads focused enough that someone else can act on them later.

Disagree on the work, not the person. It is fine to challenge a design, API, default, or release decision. It is not fine to insult, harass, shame, or pile on people.

Be careful with security, privacy, and local execution details. Do not post secrets, private data, or exploit details in public issues.

## Not Welcome Here

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or attention.
- Doxxing, leaking private information, or pressuring people to reveal private context.
- Repeated off-topic demands after maintainers have redirected the thread.
- Bad-faith participation, including deliberate misinformation or issue spam.

## Maintainer Response

Maintainers may edit titles, labels, or formatting for clarity; move conversations to a better issue; hide or delete harmful comments; lock threads that are no longer productive; or block accounts that make the project unsafe to participate in.

These actions are about keeping the project workable. They are not a promise that every issue will be accepted, implemented, or debated indefinitely.

## Reporting Conduct Problems

If something in the project space feels unsafe or seriously out of line, contact the maintainers at `hello@contenox.com`.

For security vulnerabilities, do not use public issues. Follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).
